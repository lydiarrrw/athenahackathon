How to play:

  • Select a category and click start button to start the game
  • Use arrow keys to move around the grid
  • Collect all recycible items
  • Rinse packaging for extra lives
  • Try to escape a bin bag

    Fun facts:

    • It takes up to 500 years for plastic to fully decompose.
    • The UK has more than 50,000 bottle banks, and each bank is capable of holding 3,000 bottles, yet 5 out of every 6 glass bottles are thrown away.
    • The average UK household throws away 20% of all food purchased.
    • We use 25% of the worlds fresh water supply to grow food that we will send to the landfill.
    • We throw away 1.4 million untouched bananas every day.
    • The average person throws away 74kg of food waste each year, the weight of over 1000 banana skins.
    • More than 28 billion glass bottles and jars end up in landfills every year, which is the equivalent to the Empire State Building filling up every 3 weeks.
    • You could drive a car 11 metres on the oil it takes to make 1 plastic bag.
    • Plastic consumption grows by around 4% each year in Western Europe. That might not sound a lot, but it means that within 20 years we will have doubled our current plastic use.
    • It is estimated that around 600 million tonnes of products and materials enter the UK economy each year… only 115 million tonnes of this gets recycled (source: WRAP)
    • The aluminium drinks can is the world’s most recycled packaging container – worldwide over 50% of aluminium cans are recycled.
    • Recycling one glass bottle or jar saves enough energy to power your computer for 20 minutes.
    • For every tonne of recycled newspaper, we save 17 trees.
    • It can take just seven days for old magazines and newspapers to be recycled into new ones.
    • Each UK household produces over 1 tonne of waste per year. This is the weight of a small car.
    • In less than 2 hours the UK produces enough waste to fill the Albert Hall.
    • Every year, the average dustbin contains enough unrealised energy for 500 baths, 3500 showers or 5,000 hours of television.
    • On average, every person in the UK throws away their own body weight in waste every seven weeks.
    • Up to 60% of the waste that ends up in the bin could be recycled.
